Clouds are visible masses of tiny liquid droplets or frozen crystals suspended in the atmosphere. They form when water vapor condenses around small particles like dust, salt, or pollen. [Image of the main types of clouds]

Common Cloud Types

Cirrus

High-level, thin, wispy clouds made of ice crystals. They often indicate fair weather.

Cumulus

Low-level, puffy, cotton-like clouds. They are a sign of fair weather but can grow into storm clouds (cumulonimbus).

Stratus

Low-level, gray, sheet-like clouds that often cover the entire sky and can produce drizzle.
